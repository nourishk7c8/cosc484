Brief Overview

The myths that come from ancient Egypt describe the Egyptian gods and are an important part of ancient Eqyptian religion

Deities

  • Amun - chief god of Thebes, and from New Kingdom onward, national king of Egypt
  • Anubis - god of dead, embalming, funerals, and mourning ceremonies
  • Bast - goddess of protection and cats, and the protector of Ra, his third eye.
  • Horus - god of the sky, war, and falcons; Every king, while aline, was a personification of Horus
  • Imhotep - real pharoah of 3rd dynasty; architect of step pyramid; deified as god of learning and medicine
  • Isis - goddess of magic, marriage, healing, and perfection; one of the greatest goddesses
  • Nephthys - funerary goddess
  • Ra - Sun god; also known by Amun-Ra and Akmun-Rah
  • Osiris - god of the underworld and afterlife
Only prominent deities have been listed
